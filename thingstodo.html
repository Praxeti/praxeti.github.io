Activities/Places to go in Peterborough.

Due to Peterboroughs strong heritage, there are many places you can visit to see amazing historical items. If thats not your fancy, there are still things you can do!

Peterborough Cathedral

A truly impressive example of gothic architecture, the cathedral is a must see for history enthusiasts or anyone religious.

Read more here.

Peterborough Museum & Art Gallery

One of the cities oldest buildings also houses the cities oldest objects! With over 200 thousand items in its collection to see, it's worth a visit for any historical enthusiast.
Also attached is an art gallery, which contains art from international and local artists.

Read more here.

Flag Fen

Famous for being one of the best examples of a bronze age causeway, Flag Fen is a dig site that looks to recreate what life might have been like in a bronze age village.

Read more here.

Burghley House

Burghley house is one of the largest and grandest surviving houses of the 16th century, created between 1555-87.
It now serves as a location for many events and is open for the public to explore and learn about.

Read more here.

Ferry Meadows

A lovely area to experience nature, have a walk or just sit around all day, Ferry Meadows is the regions favourite place to get outside.

Read more here.

Nene Valley Railway

The UK's #1 steam attraction, Nene Valley Railway exists to allow people to learn and experience everything there is to know about steam engines and trains.

Read more here.

ABOUT PETERBOROUGH

What you need to know.

Peterborough is located in the county of Cambridgeshire and is a Cathedeal City. It has a population of 202,110 (as of 2017), and is bordered with Northamptonshire, Lincolnshire, Fenland and Huntingdonshire.
The city is mainly known for its Cathedral and Queensgate Shopping Center.

A brief history.

Medehamstede, or Peterborough as we know it now, was guessed to have first been formed between 44-48AD for the housing of a roman legion.
It became known as Burgh in the 10th Century, but wasn't a borough until the 12th Century.
The Cathedral, which it is mainly known for, began construction around 655AD and was completed around 1287AD. It also contains the burial sites of Catherine of Aragon and Mary Queen of Scots.

What's in peterborough now?

Peterborough now has the Queensgate shopping center - one of the larger shopping centers in the country, the Cathedral which is still in current use and free to look around, Flag Fen (a bronze age village site) and many other places worth visiting like Peterborough Museum.
